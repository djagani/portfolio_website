AR VR Course-work

Project information

  • Category: UI/UX Design
  • Project date: 20 November, 2022

Completed as part of the University of Michigan's Extended Reality for Everybody Specialization, this coursework provided a comprehensive immersion into Augmented Reality (AR), Virtual Reality (VR), and Mixed Reality (MR). Throughout the specialization, I gained proficiency in XR technologies, trends, and emerging challenges. The curriculum covered user experience, storyboarding, prototyping, and the practical application of XR concepts in developing immersive applications. The hands-on experience allowed me to create captivating storyboards and low-fidelity prototypes for a virtual Greece Expedition. Additionally, I designed striking 3D models of planets using A-frame and Unity for VR and AR simulations.

Greece Expedition VR

I conducted a critical analysis of an XR application and proposed solutions for improvement. Specifically, I selected the Greece Expedition from Google Expeditions. After reviewing the original version, I identified several areas for enhancement. This VR Expedition allows users to explore a map of Greece, visit all the well-known attractions, and discover detailed information about each one. To enhance the application, I created my own XR storyboards based on the recommendations I provided. Some of the suggested features included:

  • The design provides a fully explorable map with pointers marking each attraction for easy navigation
  • Clicking on a pointer displays the attraction's name in a title box and provides the option to zoom in or read more information
  • The "info" button pops up a swipe up at the bottom of the screen that contains all the relevant information about the attraction
  • Clicking on the zoom in button provides a 360-degree inside view of the attraction or monument, which can be navigated freely
  • The design also includes a zoom out option, an info button that redirects to the information section, and a pictures tab that displays Google images of the attraction from various angles and perspectives.
  • Users can easily switch back and forth between attractions and continue exploring the map with ease and efficiency thanks to its intuitive design.

Aframe Simulations

ARearth is an innovative project that utilizes the power of marker-based Augmented Reality and Aframe to deliver an exciting 3D view of the Earth. By using a Hiro marker, I explored and interacted with the Earth in a whole new angle. With ARearth, users can view the Earth in real-time through their camera, experiencing a fully interactive and immersive view of our planet that truly brings it to life.

VRearth is an exciting and innovative project that leverages the power of Virtual Reality and Aframe to create an immersive simulation of the Earth in space. Using a 3D model of the Earth created in Aframe, VRearth transports users into an immersive VR environment where they can experience the thrill of seeing our planet from a new perspective.

The VR simulation of a crate in the forest is an immersive and captivating project that brings a 3D model of a crate to life in the context of a lush forest environment using Aframe. With the sounds of chirping birds and a variety of sound effects, users are transported to a beautiful and peaceful natural setting that feels incredibly realistic. The crate itself is rendered in stunning detail, allowing users to examine it from all angles and explore every corner of its intricate design.
